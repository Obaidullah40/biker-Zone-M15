Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

A helmet is considered old and needs replacing if it's more than five years old, shows visible signs of wear and tear like cracks, scratches, or peeling on the outer shell, has loose or damaged padding, a frayed chin strap, or if it's been involved in a crash

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

While it might be "safe" to ride a bike that's been in storage, it's not recommended to jump on it without first performing thorough checks as various components like tires, brakes, battery, and fuel system can deteriorate over time, especially if stored improperly; it's best to inspect and potentially service your bike before riding it again, especially if it's been in storage for a long period

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

When riding your bike, always follow the rules of the road, which typically include: riding on the right side of the road (in most countries), signaling your turns with hand signals, stopping at traffic lights and stop signs, yielding to pedestrians at crosswalks, wearing a helmet, using lights at night, and maintaining a safe distance from other vehicles and cyclists; always be aware of your surroundings and ride defensively, especially when encountering busy intersections or tight spaces.
